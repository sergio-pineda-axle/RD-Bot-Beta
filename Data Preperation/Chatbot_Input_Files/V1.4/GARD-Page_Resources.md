# [GARD Resources Page](https://rarediseases.info.nih.gov/resources)
General Resources for ALL rare diseases.

## [Banner: Resources]
Whether you're a patient or caregiver, GARD can help connect you with the resources and support you need to navigate life with a rare disease.
Please note that GARD provides organizations and resources for informational purposes only. Their inclusion does not reflect an endorsement by GARD or the NIH.

## [Section 1: Patient Resources]
The impacts of living with a rare disease can be widespread, affecting finances, access to care, everyday responsibilities, and mental health. The following resources may help address some of the common challenges faced by individuals living with a rare disease.

### How can you overcome barriers to accessing care?
Life with a rare disease may present unique challenges to accessing care. The following resources may help you in overcoming these barriers.

#### Health care services near you:
* [Find providers or hospitals near you (Medicare)](https://www.medicare.gov/care-compare/)
* [Find a health center near you (HRSA)](https://findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov/)

#### Transportation needs:
* [Request assistance for ground and air travel (Mercy Medical Angels)](https://www.mercymedical.org/) 
* [Arrange a public benefit flight (Air Care Alliance)](https://www.aircarealliance.org/patient-and-medical-transport/)
* [Find assistance programs for travel expenses (NeedyMeds)](https://www.needymeds.org/medtrans-branch/branch/39)

#### Disability needs:
* [Learn how to qualify for disability benefits (Social Security Administration)](https://www.ssa.gov/benefits/disability/qualify.html)
* [Discover disability resources in your area (DIAL)](https://dial.acl.gov/home)
* [Access resources for people with disabilities (National Disability Institute)](https://dial.acl.gov/home)

### How can you afford the care you need?
With or without insurance, the cost of care for rare medical conditions can place a significant financial strain on patients and families. These resources and programs may provide you financial relief.

* [Search patient assistance programs (National Organization for Rare Disorders)](https://rarediseases.org/patient-assistance-programs/)

#### Insurance Coverage 
* [Appeal insurance denials (Patient Advocate Foundation)](https://www.patientadvocate.org/download-view/tips-for-appealing-insurance-denials/)
* [Get health insurance coverage (Healthcare.gov)](https://www.healthcare.gov/get-coverage/)
* [Learn how to navigate health insurance (Global Genes)](https://globalgenes.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/Navigating-Health-Insurance_DIGITAL_spread_op.pdf)

#### Medication Cost Assistance

## [Section 2: Caregiver Resources]
Caregivers are individuals who support a person living with a rare disease. Some of their responsibilities may involve assisting with daily activities, nursing, coordinating care and making difficult decisions. The following resources may assist caregivers as they navigate these responsibilities.

### How can you address your needs as a caregiver?
As a caregiver, it is important to also care for yourself. You may at times feel overwhelmed or alone, but the following resources may help you find ways to create time for yourself, manage your emotional health, and connect with others.
 
* [Address caregiver challenges (National Alliance for Caregiving)](https://www.caregiving.org/resources/general-caregiving/)
* [Find mental health support for caregivers (Give an Hour)](https://giveanhour.org/)
* [Locate a respite center (ARCH National Respite Network & Resource Center)](https://archrespite.org/caregiver-resources/respitelocator/)

### How can you care for others?
As a caregiver, it may be challenging to find the support and resources you need to provide proper care to others. The following resources provide information and tools on home health care support, optimizing your home for caregiving, and building a community of support.

#### Apps to assist caregivers:
* [Enhance caregiver support (Caring Village)](https://caringvillage.com/)
* [Find resources using the Caregiver Toolbox (Caregiver Action Network)](https://www.caregiveraction.org/toolbox/)
* [Organize support for someone in need (Lotsa Helping Hands)](https://lotsahelpinghands.com/)
* [Track your child's milestones (CDC)](https://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/actearly/milestones-app.html)

#### Resources for caring for others:
* [Discover parent centers serving families of children with disabilities (Center for Parent Information & Resources)](https://www.parentcenterhub.org/find-your-center/)
* [Explore resources on caregiving topics (Family Caregiver Alliance)](https://www.caregiver.org/caregiver-resources/caring-for-another/)
* [Find support for families of children with special health needs (Family Voices)](https://familyvoices.org/)

## [Section 3: Resources for Your Health Care Decisions]
As you navigate life with a rare disease, you may face many difficult decisions regarding your health care. It is important to be as informed as possible when making these decisions to ensure you receive the care you need. The following resources may assist you in deciding what to do when searching for a diagnosis, communicating with your health care team, and understanding alternative forms of care.

### What if you have an undiagnosed condition or are still searching for a diagnosis?
#### General Support
* [Assist families on their diagnostic journey (U.R. Our Hope)](https://urourhope.org/)
* [Connect with a patient navigator (Undiagnosed Diseases Network Foundation](https://udnf.org/)

#### Genetic Testing Funding for Undiagnosed Conditions
* [Apply for no-cost genetic testing for rare diseases (Probably Genetic)](https://www.probablygenetic.com/)
* [Receive information and assistance for genetic testing (My Faulty Gene)](https://myfaultygene.org/)

### Where can you find tips for communicating with your provider?
Open and honest communication with your team of health care providers is critical to the diagnosis, management, and treatment of a rare disease. Knowing which questions to ask, how to prepare for an appointment, and where to turn for support will help you effectively communicate with health care providers.

#### Appointment Preparation
* [Collect your family health history (CDC)](https://www.cdc.gov/family-health-history/about/index.html)
* [Prepare for your next appointment (Society to Improve Diagnosis in Medicine)](https://www.improvediagnosis.org/patients-toolkit/)

#### Effective Communication
* [Make the most of your appointment (NIH)](https://www.nih.gov/institutes-nih/nih-office-director/office-communications-public-liaison/clear-communication/talking-your-doctor)
* [Talk with your doctor (Medline Plus)](https://medlineplus.gov/talkingwithyourdoctor.html)

#### Care Coordination
* [Learn about care coordination (Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality)](https://www.ahrq.gov/ncepcr/care/coordination.html)

### Are there alternative ways to manage your health?
Ninety-five percent of rare diseases lack an FDA-approved treatment. There may be alternatives to seek out treatment options for your rare disease. The following resources may better inform you of what options are available. 
* [Be an informed consumer (NCCIH)](https://www.nccih.nih.gov/)
* [Investigate expanded access (Reagan-Udall Foundation)](https://navigator.reaganudall.org/patients-and-caregivers)
* [Learn about "off label" drugs (FDA)](https://www.fda.gov/patients/learn-about-expanded-access-and-other-treatment-options/understanding-unapproved-use-approved-drugs-label)
