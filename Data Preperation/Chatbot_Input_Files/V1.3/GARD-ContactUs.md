## [Section 1: Get In Touch]
Are you or someone you know looking for information about rare diseases? We invite patients, family members, caregivers, and others seeking information about rare diseases to contact GARD by phone or through our contact form. Our Information Specialists provide personalized responses that are easy to understand, free of charge, and confidential.
 
GARD Information Specialists can help find information about: 

* Rare diseases 

* Finding specialists 

* Patient organizations 

* Organizations that may provide financial, disability, or travel support 

* Clinical studies

* International rare disease organizations

* Support for caregivers

* Resources for people who suspect they have an undiagnosed rare disease

We also encourage you to look at our [Resources section](https://rarediseases.info.nih.gov/resources). These resources address common needs of rare disease patients and families, including contact information for rare disease centers outside of the United States. 

Important: GARD provides information about genetic and rare diseases for your general knowledge and is not a substitute for medical advice.

## [Section 2: Crisis Support]
When living with a rare disease, it can be critical to know which resources are available in a crisis. The following organizations can offer assistance directly or can help find other emergency and non-emergency resources. The organizations and resources are listed for information purposes only. Inclusion on this list does not reflect an endorsement by GARD or the NIH.

### Where can you find support in crisis?
You do not need to navigate life with a rare disease alone. The following resources may help you find support for various challenges you may face.

#### Helplines

* [Call 988 for crisis support (988 Lifeline)](https://988lifeline.org/)

* [Search international helplines (Find a Helpline)](https://findahelpline.com/)

* [Text 741741 for crisis support (Crisis Text Line)](https://www.crisistextline.org/)

#### Mental Health Assistance

* [Find local support for a mental illness (National Alliance on Mental Illness)](https://www.nami.org/findsupport)

* [Discover the link between chronic health issues and mental illness (National Institute of Mental Health)](https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/publications/chronic-illness-mental-health)

* [Locate a substance abuse and mental health treatment center (Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration)](https://findtreatment.gov/)
